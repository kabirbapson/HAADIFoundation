Who We Are

The **Health Awareness & Anti-Drugs Initiatives Official Group (HAADI)** is a non-governmental, nonprofit organization dedicated to promoting public health, drug-free living, and community resilience across Nigeria.

Formerly known as the Drugs Free Society Initiative (DFSI), HAADI expanded its mission to include broader health awareness, community sensitization, and multi-level collaboration to address drug abuse and related social challenges. Headquartered in Gombe State, HAADI delivers structured interventions focused on prevention, awareness, support, and rehabilitation referrals—designed to fit the cultural contexts of the communities served.

Our Mission

To promote a healthier and drug-free society through awareness, prevention, counseling, and stakeholder collaboration.

Our Vision

A society empowered with knowledge, support, and opportunities to live free from drug abuse and preventable health challenges.

Why Our Work Matters

Nigeria faces one of the highest substance abuse rates in Africa. The Northeast struggles with the combined challenges of insecurity, trauma, unemployment, and limited access to mental health services—making drug abuse even more widespread.

HAADI bridges this critical gap through our community-centered model, ensuring early intervention, holistic support, and rehabilitation referral systems are accessible to those in need, transforming lives and communities.

Our Core Services & Programs

1. Community Awareness Campaigns

Public sensitization in markets, schools, and religious centers, using local radio and Hausa-language media.

2. Youth-Focused Prevention Programs

School outreach, mentorship, sports, and life skills training to offer constructive alternatives.

3. Support, Counseling & Reintegration

Initial counselling, support groups, and reintegration programs promoting community acceptance.

4. Rehabilitation Referral & Follow-up

Linkages with specialized rehabilitation centers and follow-up support.

5. Advocacy & Policy Engagement

Advocating for better public health and anti-drug policies at state and local levels.

6. Partnerships & Collaboration

Collaboration with NGOs, health facilities, and traditional institutions for sustainable development.

Our Impact & Goals

  • **120+ radio programs** aired on drug abuse prevention.
  • **500+ initial counseling sessions** conducted.

Community Impact Goals
